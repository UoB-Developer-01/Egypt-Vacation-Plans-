# How to Cancel Your Work Contract in Bahrain as an Expat Developer (Al Moyaed)

> **Disclaimer:** This guide is for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. Consult a licensed Bahraini labor lawyer for your specific situation.

---

## 1. Know Your Contract Type

Before anything else, locate your employment contract and identify:

- **Contract duration** — fixed-term or open-ended (indefinite)
- **Notice period** — typically 30–90 days for professional roles
- **Probation period** — if you are still within it (usually 3 months), different rules apply
- **Non-compete / confidentiality clauses** — check what you agreed to post-employment
- **Sponsorship type** — are you on Al Moyaed's CR (Commercial Registration) or a client's?

---

## 2. Understand Your Rights Under Bahrain Labor Law

Bahrain's **Labor Law for the Private Sector (Law No. 36 of 2012)** and its amendments govern expat employees.

| Scenario | Your Right |
|---|---|
| Resignation during probation | No end-of-service benefit; notice may be waived |
| Resignation with proper notice | End-of-service benefit accrued |
| Employer fails to pay salary | You can file a complaint immediately |
| Employer terminates without cause | You are entitled to full notice + compensation |

**End-of-Service Benefit (EOSB):**
- Half a month's wage per year for the first 3 years
- One full month's wage per year after 3 years
- Calculated on your **last basic salary** (not including allowances)

## 3. Submit Your Formal Resignation

### Step-by-step

1. **Write a formal resignation letter** (see template below).
2. **Deliver it in writing** — email to HR + hand-delivered hard copy. Keep proof of delivery.
3. **Respect your notice period** — typically stated in your contract (30, 60, or 90 days).
4. **Do not abandon work** — abandonment can void your EOSB and may cause visa/ban issues.

### Resignation Letter Template

```
[Your Full Name]
[Your CPR Number]
[Date]

To: HR Department
Al Moyaed [relevant entity name]
Manama, Kingdom of Bahrain

Subject: Formal Resignation — [Your Job Title]

Dear [HR Manager's Name],

I hereby formally resign from my position as [Job Title] at Al Moyaed,
effective [Last Working Day — calculated from today + notice period].

I will ensure a smooth handover of all responsibilities and documentation
during my notice period. Please advise on the clearance and exit procedures.

Sincerely,
[Your Signature]
[Your Name]
[Contact Number]
```

## 4. Clearance Process

During or after your notice period, you must complete a **clearance form** that typically covers:

- [ ] Return of company assets (laptop, access cards, phone)
- [ ] Handover of projects, credentials, and documentation
- [ ] Settlement of any salary advances or loans
- [ ] Sign-off from your department head, IT, and finance
- [ ] Collection of your **experience letter** and **NOC (No Objection Certificate)**

> **Important:** Request your NOC in writing. Without it, transferring to another employer in Bahrain may be delayed (though LMRA's flexible work permit system has reduced — but not eliminated — this need).

---

## 5. Visa & LMRA (Labour Market Regulatory Authority)

### Your Work Permit (Visa)

- Your work permit is tied to your employer (Al Moyaed).
- Once you resign, Al Moyaed can cancel your permit.
- You typically have **90 days after cancellation** to either transfer sponsorship or leave Bahrain.

### Options After Cancellation

| Option | Details |
|---|---|
| Transfer to a new employer | New employer initiates transfer via LMRA portal |
| Flexible Work Permit (FWP) | Self-sponsor; allows freelancing/multiple clients |
| Leave Bahrain | If not transferring, exit before permit expires |
| Visit visa / personal status | Available in limited cases while you search |

### LMRA Self-Service Portal
Check your permit status and cancellation date at:
`www.lmra.bh` → eServices → Work Permit Enquiry

## 6. Collect What You Are Owed

Before your final day, ensure you receive:

- [ ] All pending salary and allowances
- [ ] EOSB payment (or written confirmation of the amount and payment timeline)
- [ ] Reimbursement of any outstanding expenses
- [ ] Paid leave balance (unused annual leave must be paid out in cash)
- [ ] Experience/service letter
- [ ] Copy of your employment contract (you have a right to one)

---

## 7. If There Is a Dispute

### Ministry of Labour (MOL) Complaint

If Al Moyaed does not pay your dues or refuses clearance:

1. File a complaint at the **Ministry of Labour** — in person or online at `www.mlsd.gov.bh`
2. A conciliation meeting is scheduled (usually within 2–4 weeks)
3. If unresolved, the case is referred to the **Labor Courts**

### Bahrain Labour Court

- Cases are heard in Arabic; bring a translator if needed
- Most straightforward wage/EOSB cases resolve within 2–4 months
- No court fees for workers filing wage claims
- You may continue working for another employer during proceedings (if permit allows)

### Legal Aid

- **Bahrain Bar Association:** can refer you to labor lawyers
- **GFBTU (General Federation of Bahrain Trade Unions):** offers worker support
- Some law firms offer a free first consultation

```
  ☐ Note your last working day based on notice period

  ☐ Begin handover documentation
  ☐ Return company assets progressively
  ☐ Request clearance form from HR
  ☐ Request NOC and experience letter in writing

  ☐ Complete all handover sign-offs
  ☐ Confirm EOSB and final salary calculation with HR/Finance
  ☐ Collect CPR, passport (if held by employer — they must return it immediately)

  ☐ Confirm work permit status on LMRA portal
  ☐ Receive final pay (must be within 7 days of contract end per labor law)
  ☐ Initiate new permit / FWP / exit plan within 90 days
```

> **Note on Passport Withholding:** It is **illegal** for any employer in Bahrain to hold your passport. If Al Moyaed is holding your passport, demand its return in writing immediately. If refused, contact your embassy and file a complaint with the MOL.
